A Providence College graduate (B.A. '17, M.Ed), Brian played Division I soccer as #12 for the Friars, winning back-to-back BIG EAST Championships. ## Long (full bio / about page) — 260 words

Brian Edward Kennedy II was born and raised in Upland, California. Soccer was where he learned discipline first, but it was never the whole story — his mother drilled education, his community drilled character, and by the time he arrived at Providence College in Rhode Island, he came carrying all three.

Brian played Division I soccer for the Providence Friars, wearing #12 and competing at the top of the BIG EAST Conference. In his four years, the Friars brought home back-to-back BIG EAST Championship rings. He graduated in 2017 with a Bachelor of Arts and returned to complete his Master of Education — the tassel on his graduation cap read "One degree, two rings, not done yet."

After Providence, Brian came home with a clear mission: take what he'd learned at the Division I level — the discipline, the training systems, the preparation — and make it available to youth athletes in the Inland Empire who were hungry for the same exposure. The Ballers Kingdom was founded in 2019 as the vehicle for that work.

Today, Brian leads two programs under The Ballers Kingdom: private and group soccer training for serious athletes, and the Youth Venture Program, a summer and after-school entrepreneurship curriculum for ages 10–17. He also serves as Entrepreneur Ecosystem Director at AmPac, where he supports minority and underserved business owners across California, Arizona, and Nevada. The common thread across all of it: develop leaders young, and keep developing them for life. Faith. Family. Furtherment.
