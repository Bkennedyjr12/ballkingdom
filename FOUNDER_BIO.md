## Long (full bio / about page) — 360 words

Brian Kennedy Jr was born and raised in Upland, California. Soccer was where he learned discipline first, but it was never the whole story — his mother drilled education, his community drilled character, and by the time he arrived at Providence College in Rhode Island, he came carrying all three.

Brian played Division I soccer for the Providence Friars, wearing #12 and competing at the top of the BIG EAST Conference. In 2014, the program captured the BIG EAST Conference Championship and advanced to the NCAA Final Four — both firsts in program history. Two years later the Friars claimed the BIG EAST Regular Season title and reached the NCAA Elite 8. Brian earned his B.A. in Political Science in 2017 and returned to complete his M.Ed. in Guidance & Counseling in 2019, earning Athletics All-Academic Honor Roll recognition along the way. The tassel on his graduation cap read "One degree, two rings, not done yet."

After Providence, Brian came home with a clear mission: take what he'd learned at the Division I level — the discipline, the training systems, the preparation — and make it available to youth athletes in the Inland Empire. The Ballers Kingdom was founded in 2019 as the vehicle for that work. Today, he leads two programs under it: private and group soccer training for serious athletes, and the Youth Venture Program, a summer and after-school entrepreneurship curriculum for ages 10–17.

Off the field, Brian serves as Entrepreneur Ecosystem Executive Director at AmPac Business Capital — the SBA's first faith-based certified lender and the #1 SBA 504 Lender in the OC/IE District (2017) — where he helps deploy $1B+ in loans and support 1,500+ businesses across California, Arizona, and Nevada, including $15M+ in PPP administered during the pandemic. In 2024, Assembly Majority Leader Emerita Eloise Gómez Reyes recognized him with the California District 50 · 30 Under 30 Award. That same year, he founded The Entrepreneur Ecosystem, a 501(c)(3) advancing small business support for underserved communities. He also serves as Student Success Counselor at Los Angeles College of Music. The common thread: develop leaders young, and keep developing them for life. Faith. Family. Furtherment.
